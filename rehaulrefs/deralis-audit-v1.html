In practice

What an audit actually looks like.

The five pieces of a decision brief, walked through on a composite example.

Situation

A French SME, about thirty staff, coordinating field operations across multiple sites. Profitable, growing, run day-to-day on WhatsApp, email, and three separate SaaS tools that didn't talk to each other.

What was breaking

Nobody could answer "where is this right now?" without making two phone calls. The same information was being entered into three places. The operations lead was spending a full day a week reconciling the gaps manually.

What the audit clarified

The problem was not the tools. The problem was that no single place held the current state of a job. Adding a fourth tool would have made it worse. The fix was one thin layer on top of the existing tools, not a replacement of any of them.

What the first build should be

A single status surface showing every active job, its current stage, and who owns the next step. Pulling from the existing tools, not replacing them. Scoped to ship in weeks, not months.

What was explicitly ruled out

A full operations platform. A mobile app. A CRM replacement. Each of those was a real temptation the audit made it easier to say no to, because the brief named them and explained why they were wrong for this operation right now.
