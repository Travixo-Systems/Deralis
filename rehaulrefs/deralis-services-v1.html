Core services

Three sequential stages. Each one earns the next.

01

Systems Audit

Who it's for Businesses experiencing friction in their operations
Typical timeline One session, written brief delivered after
Investment €300 · deducted if the project proceeds

Before anything is built, I map how your operation actually works today. We walk through the tools you use, the people involved, where information lives, where it gets stuck, and where the friction is costing you time or money.

The audit produces a written brief that defines what to build, why it matters, and in what order. If the brief identifies a project worth doing, the €300 is deducted from the build cost. If it identifies that nothing needs to be built right now, I will tell you that directly.

02

Custom System

Who it's for Teams running operations manually or across fragmented tools
Typical timeline Defined after the audit, based on real scope
Investment Scope-dependent, defined after the audit

The core build. One system that shows where work is, what is blocked, and what needs to happen next. Designed around the specific operation the audit mapped, not around a template.

This usually replaces a combination of spreadsheets, disconnected SaaS tools, and informal processes that live in someone's head. The goal is a single source of truth the team actually uses, not a dashboard that gets ignored after launch.

Custom System work is always scoped against the audit brief. The brief defines what gets built, what does not, and what comes later as Extensions.

03

Extensions

Who it's for Clients with a working system who need additional capabilities
Typical timeline Defined per extension, smaller than core build
Investment Scope-dependent, defined after the audit

Extensions are automation, integrations, and additional workflows built on top of a working foundation. Extensions only happen after a Custom System exists and is being used. Building extensions on top of a system that does not yet work creates more problems than it solves.

Common extensions include connecting the system to existing tools the team already uses, automating recurring tasks that currently happen by hand, and adding reporting or visibility layers for stakeholders who need to see the data without operating it directly.

Extensions are always defined against the original audit brief, with each extension scoped individually.

Optional. Available after a Custom System is delivered.

This covers updates, performance monitoring, bug fixes, system documentation, and on-demand support. New features and significant additions are scoped separately as Extensions.

Common questions

Questions clients ask before booking the audit.

How much does a typical project cost?

Every system is different and the cost depends on what your operation actually needs, not on a fixed package. Before quoting anything, I run a Systems Audit to map your operation and define what needs to be built. The audit costs €300 and produces a written brief. If the project proceeds, the €300 is deducted from the build cost.

Do you work with clients outside France?

Yes. I work remotely with clients across France, the UK, and the US. Communication is in French or English depending on what works for you. Audit sessions and project work happen over video calls, with the deliverables and the system itself accessible from anywhere.

Can you take over an existing project?

Sometimes. The Systems Audit covers existing systems too, not just operations that have never been digitized. If the existing system is salvageable, the brief will define what to keep, what to rebuild, and what to deprecate. If it is not salvageable, the brief will say so, and you can decide whether to start over or keep what you have.

How long does a typical project take?

Defined after the audit. The brief includes a realistic timeline for the work it scopes, based on the complexity of your operation and the depth of the build. Projects can range from a few weeks for a focused Custom System to several months for a deeper engagement with multiple Extensions. The audit is the only stage with a fixed timeline: one session, brief delivered shortly after.
